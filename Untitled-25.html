About Mental Health

Understanding mental health is the first step toward wellness and recovery.

What is Mental Health?

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make healthy choices.

Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood. Over the course of your life, if you experience mental health problems, your thinking, mood, and behavior could be affected.

Essential Yoga Poses for Mental Wellness

Child's Pose (Balasana)

A restorative pose that calms the nervous system and reduces stress.

Benefits: Reduces anxiety, calms the mind, relieves tension

Hold: 1-3 minutes

Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)

Builds confidence and mental strength while improving focus.

Benefits: Boosts confidence, improves concentration, builds resilience

Hold: 30 seconds each side

Tree Pose (Vrikshasana)

Enhances balance and promotes mental stability and focus.

Benefits: Improves focus, enhances balance, calms the mind

Hold: 30-60 seconds each side

Legs Up the Wall (Viparita Karani)

A gentle inversion that promotes relaxation and reduces anxiety.

Benefits: Reduces anxiety, improves circulation, promotes sleep

Hold: 5-15 minutes

Cat-Cow Pose (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana)

Gentle spinal movement that releases tension and calms the mind.

Benefits: Releases tension, improves mood, enhances mindfulness

Repeat: 5-10 rounds

Corpse Pose (Savasana)

Deep relaxation pose that integrates the benefits of practice.

Benefits: Deep relaxation, stress relief, mental clarity

Hold: 5-20 minutes

Pranayama: Breathing for Mental Health

4-7-8 Breathing

Inhale for 4, hold for 7, exhale for 8. Activates the relaxation response.

Best for: Anxiety, insomnia, stress relief

Practice: 4-8 cycles

Alternate Nostril Breathing

Balances the nervous system and harmonizes brain hemispheres.

Best for: Mental clarity, emotional balance

Practice: 5-10 minutes

Box Breathing

Equal counts for inhale, hold, exhale, hold. Promotes focus and calm.

Best for: Focus, stress management, PTSD

Practice: 5-10 minutes

Getting Started with Yoga for Mental Health

For Beginners

1. Start with 10-15 minutes daily
2. Focus on gentle, restorative poses
3. Use online classes or apps for guidance
4. Listen to your body and don't force poses
5. Create a consistent practice routine

Best Times to Practice

Morning (6-8 AM)

Energizing poses to start the day with clarity and focus

Lunch Break (12-1 PM)

Quick stress-relief sequences to reset your day

Evening (6-8 PM)

Gentle, restorative poses to unwind and prepare for sleep

Remember

Yoga is not about perfect poses - it's about the connection between mind, body, and breath. Even 5 minutes of mindful movement can make a difference in your mental well-being. Be patient with yourself and celebrate small progress.
