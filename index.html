Why mental health awareness?

Mental health affects how we think, feel and act. Awareness helps remove stigma, encourages early help-seeking, and builds supportive communities. This site provides practical information and connections to professional support.

  • Educational guides and signs to watch for
  • Practical self-care routines
  • Local & national resources and helplines

Common signs someone may be struggling

Recognising changes early can lead to timely help. Signs may include:

Emotional

  • Persistent sadness or irritability
  • Feeling overwhelmed or numb

Behavioural

  • Withdrawal from friends or activities
  • Changes in sleep or eating patterns

Cognitive

  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Hopeless thoughts

Physical

  • Chronic tiredness oraches
  • Unexplained pains

Self-care techniques you can try today

Daily routine

Create small, consistent habits: regular sleep, nutritious meals, and short walks.

Mindfulness

Try breathing exercises, grounding techniques, or 5–10 minutes of meditation.

Connect

Reach out to a friend, join a supportive group, or talk to a counselor.

Helplines & resources

Immediate help and trusted organisations:

  • Local crisis line: If you are in immediate danger, call your country's emergency number.
  • National helpline (India): KIRAN Mental Health Helpline — 1800-599-0019 (verify before publishing).
  • International: World Health Organization, International Association for Suicide Prevention
  • Online therapy: try directories like Psychology Today or your local healthcare portal.
